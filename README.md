I sometimes wonder: do we really need to explore every single path?
Of course, the unforgettable experience of playing the original Diablo for the first time is precious.
However, decades have passed, and there are now certain parts that feel undeniably inefficient.

Another thought I had is that most players, myself included, eventually start with a Sorceress.
Even if you made other classes five times stronger, people would still choose the Sorceress simply because of Teleport.
This is why many modders try to balance the game by adding penalties to Teleport or introducing other mechanics to restrain it.

But I began to wonder—is Teleport really the root of the problem?
I believe the craving for Teleport is at its peak when you are repeatedly farming maps like the Catacombs.
If we can alleviate those suffocating aspects of the game, it puts everyone on a much more even playing field.
Players will no longer feel blindly tied to Teleport, leading to a far more enjoyable experience overall.
